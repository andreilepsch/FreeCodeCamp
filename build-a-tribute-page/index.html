Brasilia capital of Brazil

in the timeline

Brasilia is the federal capital of Brazil and seat of government of the Federal District

Brasília's Timeline

  • 1751 - The Marquis of Pombal expressed the possibility of transferring the capital of Brazil, then a Portuguese colony, to the interior. He commissioned the first geographical map of Goias highlighting the strategic value of the Central Plateau.
  • 1789 - The Conspirators Miners led by Tiradentes, planned to install the new capital of Brazil in São João del Rei. They aimed to a safer location and the settlement of the interior.
  • 1810 - The Board and Chancellor Veloso de Oliveira warned the Prince Regent John 6 for the convenience of changing the capital to a place "are mild, gentle and free of confusing throng of people indiscriminately accumulated."
  • 1822 - After the Proclamation of Independence, Palmiro Menezes, São Paulo deputy, proposed the creation of a Central Province so that there was constuída the Empire of Capital with the name of Pedrália.
  • 1823 - Jose Bonifacio, the Patriaca Independence, proposed to the Constituent Assembly that the Empire Capital is transferred to the County of Prince Paracatu, Minas Gerais. Suggests the name of Brasilia or Petropolis
  • 1831-14 May. - The first project for the choice of a central part of the country to host the future capital is presented by Mr João Cândido of God and Silva (PA).
  • 1833-04-September. - The Italian priest Giovanni Bosco recounts a dream he had, pointing out the emergence of a new civilization: "Among the degrees 15:20, there was a fairly wide and long land within which started from a point where it formed a lake. And then a voice said suddenly "When you come dig the hidden Minerals in the middle of these mountains, suggest the Promised Land, flowing with milk and honey. It will be an inconceivable richness. "
  • 1891-24-February. - The first Constitution of the Republic, in its Article 3 provides that an area of ​​14.4 km2 in the Central Plateau of the Republic, shall belong to the Union and in the area to establish the future federal capital.
  • 1892-17 de Maio - President Floriano Peixoto is the Explorer Commission Planalto Central Brazil, headed by Belgian engineer and astronomer Luiz Cruls to study and demarcate the area of ​​the new capital. July 9 - The Explorer Commission Planalto Central Brazil, with 22 members of the Rio de Janeiro to start the Central Plateau studies. There were approximately 4000 km trip on the back of mules and horses
  • 1893 - The Brazil map indicates for the first time, the Plateau of Goiás, a rectangle with the inscription "Federal District Future", known as Quadrilateral Cruls.
  • 1894-07 de Maio - Luiz Cruls publishes report on the studies in the Central Plateau. From the report, the Commission of the New Capital Union Studies is created.
  • 1922-07 of September - The cornerstone of the future capital of the United States of Brazil is launched next Planaltina (today, one of the administrative divisions of the Federal District) during the celebrations of the centenary of the Independence of Brazil.
  • 1946 - With the fall of the Estado Novo, the new constitution decrees that "the Union's Capital will be transferred to the Central Plateau." President Eurico Gaspar Dutra appoints a technical committee under the chairmanship of Djalma Poli Coelho Marshal to conduct studies location.
  • 1948-21 Aug - The committee chaired by Djalma Marshal Polli Coelho suggested as the location of the new capital the same territory chosen by the Commission Explorer Plateau Central Brazil in 1892
  • 1953 - Decree of Getulio Vargas created the Commission Location.
  • 1955 - April 04 - In rally for presidential candidacy in Jataí, Goiás, JK promises to follow given the Transitory Provisions of the Constitution and build Brasilia. 03 October - JK wins elections with 33.82% of the vote.
  • 1956 - Built Novacap, Urbanizadora Company's new capital, public company responsible for planning and executing the construction of the new capital, in Goiás cerrado region. 02 October - Pediatrician and pioneer Ernesto Silva receives JK in the cerrado and shows the map of the region, a result of the work of the Central Plateau Explorer Committee and the Location of New Federal Capital Commission. 22 October - Beginning of construction of the boards Palace, Catetinho, designed by Oscar Niemeyer, the official residence of the president during the works. November 10 - Catetinho Inauguration.
  • 1957-16 of March - Lúcio Costa architect won the competition and won the Pilot Plan Cr $ 1 million. April 2 - Airport Opening with runway 2400 meters and modern passenger station. April 3 - Start of works to the Alvorada Palace. May 3 - Don Carlos Carmel prays the first mass of Brasilia in the presence of 15 thousand people. May 4 - Inauguration of the Don Bosco Chapel. The small oratory, where the faithful can see a picture of Don Bosco, was the first work of masonry Brasilia. October 1 - JK sanctions the law setting the date of April 21, 1960 to move the capital from Rio de Janeiro to Brasilia.
  • 1958-04 de Janeiro - Start of construction of the National Congress. February 1 - highway Inauguration Belém-Brasília. December - End of the works of the Congress and the Supreme Court.
  • 1960-17 of April - Israel Pinheiro, President of Novacap, is appointed mayor of Brasilia. First phone call to Rio de Janeiro. April 21 - Brasilia was inaugurated, with the simultaneous installation of the three branches of government.
